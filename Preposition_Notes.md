# Preposition — Complete Notes (with Examples)

## 1. What is a Preposition?

- **Pre + Position** → a word *placed before* a noun or pronoun.
- Prepositions are **"relative words"** — they show the relationship (सम्बन्ध) between a noun/pronoun and some other word in the sentence.
- The word that comes **after** a preposition is called its **object**, and an object is always a **noun or pronoun**.
- A preposition can relate its object to a noun, a verb, an adjective, an adverb — basically any part of speech.
- **Position in a sentence:** a preposition can appear at the beginning, middle, or end of a sentence.

**Examples:**
1. He is fond **of** music. → "of" relates the noun "music" to the adjective "fond."
2. The book is **on** the table. → "on" relates "table" to the verb "is" (position).
3. What are you looking **at**? → preposition at the end; object "what" has moved to the front, and "you" (not "what") is the subject of the sentence.

---

## 2. Three Categories of Preposition

1. Preposition of **Time**
2. Preposition of **Place**
3. Preposition of **Direction**

**Examples (same preposition, different category):**
1. He will come **in** the morning. → Time
2. The students are sitting **in** the room. → Place
3. He is standing **in** the queue moving towards the gate. → context can shift meaning between place/direction

## 3. PREPOSITIONS OF TIME

### A. SINCE vs FOR

| | Meaning | Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Since** | Definite/fixed **point of time** | since Monday, since 2016 |
| **For** | **Length/duration** of time | for two hours, for a month |

**Examples — Since (point of time):**
1. He has been absent **since** Monday.
2. She has lived here **since** 2014.
3. I have known him **since** childhood.

**Examples — For (duration):**
1. He has been ill **for** two weeks.
2. They travelled **for** four hours.
3. She waited **for** a month before replying.

- **No plural with "since"** — if plural, use **for**, even with "last."

**Examples:**
1. He has been absent **since last week**. ✅ (singular, fixed point)
2. He has been absent **for the last two weeks**. ✅ (plural → duration, not "since")
3. She has been ill **for the last three days**. ✅ (plural → for, not since)

- **"For a month"** is not definite (any month could fit) → duration → **for**.

**Examples:**
1. I will complete this **for a month**. (could be any month)
2. He worked there **for a year**. (any year-length period)
3. We will travel **for a week**. (any week)

- Exception: **"since ancient times"** — plural, but represents fixed historical stages, not duration → still **since**.

**Examples:**
1. This custom has existed **since ancient times**.
2. People have followed this ritual **since ancient times**.
3. This tradition has continued **since ancient times**, not for a duration.

- **"Time immemorial"** — takes **since** traditionally; Modern English also allows **from**. Both correct.

**Examples:**
1. This practice has continued **since time immemorial**. ✅
2. This practice has continued **from time immemorial**. ✅
3. Both sentences describe an age-old custom and are equally correct.

### B. SINCE vs FROM

- **Since** — used with **Present Perfect** tense.
- **From** — used with **simple past** tense, not perfect.

**Examples:**
1. He **has been** absent **since** Monday. (perfect tense + since)
2. He **was** absent **from** Monday **to** Friday. (simple past + from...to)
3. She **has worked** here **since** 2018. (perfect tense + since)

---

### C. AT / IN / ON (Time)

**AT** — exact/precise point of time (dawn, sunrise, sunset, noon, midnight, exact clock time)

**Examples:**
1. The sun rises **at** dawn.
2. It is exactly midnight **at** 12 o'clock.
3. The meeting starts **at** 10 o'clock.

**IN** — a duration/longer stretch of time (morning, afternoon, evening, month, year)

**Examples:**
1. He goes for a walk **in** the morning.
2. She was born **in** 2016.
3. The event took place **in** January.

**ON** — specific day or date (always "on" with a date, whichever order it appears)

**Examples:**
1. My birthday is **on** the 16th of March.
2. We will meet **on** Monday.
3. The function is scheduled **on** Monday evening.

**Special case — NIGHT:** takes both AT and IN

**Examples:**
1. He sleeps late **at** night. (habitual/general)
2. She goes for a walk **at** night. (routine)
3. The inaugural ceremony was held **in** the night. (a specific/particular night)

### D. Festivals

- **AT** — no fixed date; **ON** — fixed date; **"on the occasion of"** always.

**Examples:**
1. He will come **at** Holi. (no fixed date festival)
2. Christmas falls **on** the 25th of December. (fixed date)
3. He will come **on the occasion of** Holi. / He will come **on the eve of** Holi (the evening before).

### E. WITHIN vs IN

- **Within** = before/up to a deadline; **In** = takes that much time exactly.

**Examples:**
1. I will return the book **within** five days. (before 5 days are up)
2. I will return the book **in** five days. (it will take the full 5 days)
3. I will complete the work **within** an hour. vs I will complete the work **in** an hour.

### F. DURING vs WHILE

- **During** + noun (event); **While** + verb-ing (action) or clause.

**Examples:**
1. Many people died **during** the war.
2. He got hurt **while playing** cricket.
3. He was studying **while** she was watching a movie.

### G. TILL vs UNTIL

- Interchangeable; both preposition or conjunction.

**Examples:**
1. I will wait **till** Monday.
2. I will wait **until** Monday.
3. I will not leave **until/till** he comes.

### H. BY (time)

**Examples:**
1. I will come **by** 2 o'clock.
2. There is a temple **by** the lake. (beside)
3. The parcel was delivered **by** the highwayman. (agent)

## 4. PREPOSITIONS OF PLACE

### A. AT vs IN

**Examples:**
1. The postman was standing **at** the door step. (exact point)
2. The students are sitting **in** the room. (larger area)
3. He was waiting **at** the bus stop.

### B. ON (surface) — table/restaurant example

**Examples:**
1. The book is **on** the table. (resting on surface)
2. The guests were waiting **at** the table. (not "on" — sitting at, not resting on)
3. The guests were waiting **in** the restaurant. (large hall)

### C. Street / house numbers

**Examples:**
1. He lives **at** 30 Park Street. (exact number)
2. He lives **in** Park Street. (general locality)
3. The office is **at** 140, MG Road, but **in** MG Road generally.

### D. Countries — IN vs TO

**Examples:**
1. He lives **in** India. (staying)
2. He went **to** India. (movement)
3. She travelled **to** London last year.

### E. GO + place

**Examples:**
1. He goes **to** the office every day.
2. She went **to** the market.
3. They went **to** London for holidays.

### F. ARRIVE — AT vs IN

**Examples:**
1. He arrived **in** India yesterday. (large place)
2. He arrived **at** the station. (small/specific place)
3. He arrived **at** his destination.

### G. REACH, VISIT, ENTER, ORDER, ATTACK — Verb vs Noun

**Examples — as VERB (no preposition):**
1. He **visited** India last year.
2. He **reached** the station on time.
3. He **ordered** the book online.

**Examples — as NOUN (preposition used):**
1. His **visit to** the zoo was memorable.
2. His **order for** the book was placed yesterday.
3. The **attack on** the enemy was sudden.

### H. ABROAD

**Examples:**
1. He went **abroad** last month. (no preposition, adverb)
2. He went **to** New York. (specific place → preposition needed)
3. She studied **abroad** for two years.

### I. ENTER — place vs joining

**Examples:**
1. He **entered** the room. (physical place, no preposition)
2. The two countries **entered into** an alliance.
3. They **entered into** a discussion about the treaty.

### J. ON vs UPON vs ONTO

**Examples:**
1. The cat is sitting **on** the table. (static, already there)
2. The cat jumped **upon** the mouse. (falling/pouncing down)
3. He climbed **onto** the horse. (movement upward)

### K. OVER/ABOVE vs UNDER/BELOW

**Examples:**
1. The fan is **over** my head. (height)
2. His performance was **above** average. (level)
3. The cat is sitting **under** the table. (height) / Her marks were **below** average. (level)

### L. SPREAD — takes OVER

**Examples:**
1. Spread the cloth **over** the table.
2. He ruled **over** a vast empire.
3. The fog spread **over** the entire city.

### M. IN vs INTO

**Examples:**
1. The pen is **in** the box. (already inside, no change)
2. He jumped **into** the river. (movement, medium change)
3. Translate the passage **from** Hindi **into** English. (complete change)

## 5. PREPOSITIONS OF DIRECTION

### A. AT — exact target

**Examples:**
1. The hunter aimed **at** the tiger.
2. He shot **at** the bird but missed.
3. She threw the ball **at** him.

### B. TOWARDS vs TO

**Examples:**
1. The snake is coming **towards** you. (direction only)
2. He turned **towards** the mirror.
3. She walked **to** the station. (direction + arrival)

### C. ALONG vs ACROSS vs THROUGH

**Examples:**
1. He walked **along** the road.
2. She walked **across** the road to reach the other side.
3. The train passed **through** the tunnel.

---

## 6. BETWEEN vs AMONG vs AMONGST

**Examples — Between (2, with "and"):**
1. Choose **between** tea **and** coffee.
2. There was a fight **between** Ram **and** Shyam.
3. Distribute the sweets **between** the two children.

**Examples — Among (3+, no "and"):**
1. Distribute the sweets **among** the students.
2. There was confusion **among** the members.
3. He sat **among** his friends.

**Examples — Exception words always taking "between" (even 3+):**
1. There is a **difference between** India, England, and Russia.
2. Choose **between** red, blue, and green.
3. There is a **connection between** the three companies.

---

## 7. BY vs WITH (Instrument vs Agent)

**Examples — With (instrument):**
1. He cut his finger **with** a knife.
2. She was killed **with** a dagger.
3. He wrote the letter **with** a pen.

**Examples — By (agent):**
1. He was killed **by** the highwayman.
2. The novel was written **by** the author.
3. The window was broken **by** the boy.

---

## 8. OF — Made of vs Made from

**Examples — Made of (physical, reversible):**
1. The table is **made of** wood.
2. The cup is **made of** glass.
3. The ring is **made of** gold.

**Examples — Made from (chemical, irreversible):**
1. Curd is **made from** milk.
2. Flour is **made from** wheat.
3. Wine is **made from** grapes.

---

## 9. DIED OF / FROM / IN / BY / FOR

**Examples:**
1. He **died of** cancer. (disease)
2. He **died from** over-eating. (external cause)
3. He **died in** an accident. / He **died by** violence. / He **died for** his country.

---

## 10. Mode of Transport

**Examples:**
1. He goes to the office **on foot**. (never "by foot")
2. He travels **by** train every day.
3. He travels **in** the Rajdhani Express. (specific named train → "in," not "by")

---

## 11. BESIDE vs BESIDES

**Examples:**
1. The temple is **beside** the lake. (next to)
2. **Besides** English, he speaks French. (in addition to)
3. **Besides** food, medicines were also sent.

---

## Quick-Reference Summary Table

| Concept | Rule |
|---|---|
| Since | Fixed point of time; no plural; used with perfect tense |
| For | Duration; used with plural time-words |
| From | Point of time, used with simple past |
| At (time) | Exact point: dawn, noon, midnight, clock time |
| In (time) | Duration: morning, month, year |
| On (time) | Specific day/date |
| Night | At night = habitual; In the night = specific occasion |
| last/next/every + day | No preposition |
| Festivals | At = no fixed date; On = fixed date; "on the occasion of" |
| Within vs In | Within = before deadline; In = takes that much time |
| During vs While | During + noun (event); While + verb-ing (action) |
| Till/Until | Interchangeable; preposition or conjunction |
| At vs In (place) | At = exact point; In = larger area |
| Arrive at/in | At = small place; In = large place |
| Visit/enter/reach/order/attack | No preposition as verb; preposition when used as noun |
| Enter into | "Into" only when joining agreement/discussion, not physical place |
| On/Upon/Onto | On = static; Upon = falls onto; Onto = climbs/jumps up onto |
| Over/Under vs Above/Below | Height (non-spread) → over/under; Level (spread/comparison) → above/below |
| In vs Into | In = no state change; Into = change of state/medium |
| Towards vs To | Towards = direction only; To = direction + arrival |
| Along/Across/Through | Along = parallel; Across = side to side; Through = via enclosed space |
| Between vs Among | Between = 2 (+"and"); Among = 3+ (no "and") |
| Choose/differentiate/connection/difference/link/relationship | Always "between," even for 3+ |
| With vs By | With = instrument; By = agent/doer |
| Made of vs Made from | Of = physical/reversible; From = chemical/irreversible |
| Died of/from/in/by/for | Of = disease; From = external cause; In = accident; By = violence; For = cause/country |
| On foot | Never "by foot" |
| By vs In (vehicle) | By = general; In = specific named vehicle |
| Beside vs Besides | Beside = next to; Besides = in addition to |
