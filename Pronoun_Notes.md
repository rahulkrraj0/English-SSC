# Pronoun — Complete Notes

## 1. Pronoun क्या है?
- **Pronoun** वह शब्द है जो **Noun के बदले** आता है।
- Use करने का कारण: **In order to avoid the repetition of noun** (नाउन बार-बार repeat न हो)।
  - Example: *Ram is a good boy. Ram goes to school on time. Ram is very brilliant. Ram helps others.*
  → बार-बार "Ram" की जगह: *He* is a good boy. *He* goes to school on time. *He* is very brilliant. *He* helps others.

- **Pronoun का काम**: वही काम जो Noun करता है — **Sentence में Subject या Object बनना**.
  - Example: *He helps me.* → He = Subject, me = Object (दोनों Pronoun हैं)

---

## 2. Noun के बदले किस Pronoun का प्रयोग

| Noun Type | Pronoun |
|---|---|
| Male person | He |
| Female person | She |
| Non-living thing | It |
| Animal (generally) | It |
| Animal (story/fable में human characteristics देने के लिए) | He/She |
| Gender clear न हो (doctor/teacher/soldier आदि) | He (Male को Man/default माना जाता है) |
| Baby/Child (छोटे बच्चे, gender clear न हो तब भी) | It (पर इसका मतलब यह नहीं कि बच्चा animal है) |
| India/Country (motherland sense में) | She (अन्यथा) It |

**Important:** *It* का Plural **"They"** होता है, **"Its" नहीं**।
- It → They (Plural)
- Its = It का **Possessive** (जैसे He का His, She का Her)

## 4. Personal Pronoun

- **First, Second, Third Person** से बनता है:
  - First Person: I, We
  - Second Person: You
  - Third Person: He, She, It, They

- **इट (It) कभी Personal, कभी Impersonal होता है:**
  - जब **It Subject या Object का काम करे** → **Personal Pronoun**
    - *The lion killed the boy. It killed the cat.* (It = Subject → Personal)
  - जब **It Subject/Object का काम न करे**, बल्कि केवल sentence शुरू करने के लिए (Dummy Subject) प्रयोग हो → **Impersonal Pronoun**
    - *It is I who help Ram.* (यहाँ It किसी को introduce कर रहा है — Impersonal)
    - Impersonal Pronoun से हम अपने Subject को **introduce** करते हैं।

---

## 5. Cases of Personal Pronoun

तीन Cases होते हैं:
1. **Subjective Case** (Nominative Case) — जब Pronoun Subject का काम करे
2. **Objective Case** (Accusative/Genitive भी कहते हैं यहाँ objective को) — जब Pronoun Object का काम करे
3. **Possessive Case** — अधिकार (ownership) बताने के लिए

### Table:

| Person | Subjective | Objective |
|---|---|---|
| I | I | Me |
| We | We | Us |
| You | You | You |
| He | He | Him |
| She | She | Her |
| It | It | It |
| They | They | Them |

### Possessive के दो प्रकार:
1. **Possessive Adjective** — इसके बाद हमेशा एक Noun आता है
   - my, our, your, his, her, its, their
   - *This is my book.* (Noun "book" चाहिए ही)
2. **Possessive Pronoun** — इसके बाद Noun की जरूरत नहीं
   - mine, ours, yours, his, hers, its, theirs
   - *This book is mine.*

**Trick:** अगर Possessive के बाद Noun अटका हुआ लगे (अधूरा वाक्य) → Possessive Adjective. अगर वाक्य पूरा लगे बिना Noun के → Possessive Pronoun.

## 6. Common Errors related to Personal Pronoun (Exam Focus)

### (a) Conjunction Rule
Conjunction (and, or आदि) हमेशा **Subject को Subject से** और **Object को Object से** जोड़ता है (कभी mix नहीं करता)।
- ❌ *For you and I* → ✅ *For you and me* (Preposition "for" के बाद Object चाहिए)
- ✅ *Ram and I will do the work* (Subject को Subject से जोड़ा)

### (b) Reflexive Pronoun कभी Subject नहीं बन सकता
- ❌ *Myself Pradeep* → ✅ *I am Pradeep / My name is Pradeep*
- ❌ *Ram and myself will do the work* → ✅ *Ram and I will do the work*

### (c) "Let" के बाद हमेशा Objective Case
- *Let him do it.* (Let he ❌)

### (d) "It is / It was" के बाद हमेशा Subjective Case
- ❌ *It's me* → ✅ *It is I*
- ❌ *It was him* → ✅ *It was he*
- कारण: "It is/It was" impersonal pronoun का काम करता है और यह Object को नहीं, Subject को introduce करता है।

### (e) "If I were" पैटर्न (Comparison Rule)
Subject का comparison हमेशा Subject से होगा, Object का Object से।
- *If I were he, I would help him.* (Subject से Subject का comparison)
- *I mistook Ram for Shyam.* (Object से Object का comparison — Ram व Shyam दोनों Object)

### (f) "Than" के बाद Subjective या Objective — Meaning पर निर्भर करता है
- *I know you better than he (does).* → Subject का comparison Subject से
- *I know you better than him.* → Object का comparison Object से (मतलब अलग हो जाता है)
- दोनों sentences grammatically सही हैं पर meaning अलग-अलग है।

## 7. Reflexive & Emphatic (Emphasizing) Pronoun

- दोनों में "self/selves" वाले शब्द आते हैं: myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, ourselves, yourselves, themselves
- **Reflexive** — जब Verb **के बाद** आए, और काम करने वाला (Doer) व पाने वाला (Receiver) **एक ही व्यक्ति** हो
  - *I did the work myself.* (Doer = Receiver = I)
- **Emphatic** — जब Verb **से पहले** आए (जोर देने के लिए / Emphasis डालने के लिए)
  - *I myself did the work.*

### Reflexive Pronoun के Rules:
1. Reflexive Pronoun **कभी भी Sentence का Subject नहीं बन सकता**।
2. Reflexive तभी use होगा जब **Doer और Receiver Same Person** हों।
   - *I introduced myself to the chairman.* (सही — Doer=Receiver=I)
   - *I introduced him to the chairman.* (सही — अलग व्यक्ति को introduce किया, Reflexive नहीं)

### कुछ Verbs जो हमेशा अपने साथ Reflexive Pronoun लेते हैं (जब meaning "अपने आप पर" हो):
- **Avail** (हमेशा + Reflexive + "of" preposition) → *He availed himself of the opportunity.*
- **Enjoy** — पर ध्यान दें: तभी लगेगा जब खुद को enjoy किया हो
  - *I enjoyed myself at the party.* (खुद को enjoy किया → Reflexive)
  - *I enjoyed the party.* (party को enjoy किया → No Reflexive)
- **Absent** — जब Verb का काम करे → Reflexive
  - *He absented himself from school.*
  - पर जब "absent" Adjective का काम करे → No Reflexive (*He was absent from school*)
- **Resign** — दो meanings:
  1. Job से resign करना → **No Reflexive** (*He resigned from the job*)
  2. "समर्पण करना / Surrender" के meaning में → **Reflexive** (*He resigned himself to his fate*)
- **Adapt** (+ Reflexive + "to") → *She adapted herself to the new environment.*
- **Reconcile** (+ Reflexive) → *He reconciled himself to their demand.*
- **Pride** (+ Reflexive + "on") 
- **Avenge**, **Amuse**, **Disguise** (अपने आप को छुपाना), **Prostrate** (दंडवत करना)
- **Hurt** — *He hurt himself.*

**Rule:** ऊपर दिए सभी शब्दों के साथ Reflexive तभी लगेगा जब वे वाक्य में **Verb** का काम कर रहे हों, **Adjective** का काम कर रहे हों तो नहीं लगेगा।

## 8. Reciprocal Pronoun

- **Each other** → दो (2) व्यक्तियों/चीज़ों के लिए
- **One another** → दो से ज्यादा (more than two) के लिए
- Example: *The two cats fought with each other.*
- Example: *All the students of the class helped one another.*

---

## 9. Order of Pronouns (Arrangement Rule — 2-3-1 / 1-2-3)

### Normal (Positive) Sentence में:
**Order: 2nd Person → 3rd Person → 1st Person (2-3-1)**
- *You, he and I will do the work.*

### Negative Sentence में या जब कोई गलती Accept कर रहे हों:
**Order: 1st Person → 2nd Person → 3rd Person (1-2-3)**
(विनम्रता से पहले खुद को जिम्मेदार ठहराना)
- *We three are responsible for the mistake.*
- *I, you and he are responsible for the mistake.*

### जब Sentence में Plural Pronoun (we/you/they) पहले से आ जाए:
वहाँ भी **1-2-3** क्रम रखें
- *We, you and they should cooperate.*

---

## 10. Pronoun Agreement (Number/Person) — "Nearest Subject" Rule
जब Sentence में एक से ज्यादा subjects हों (I, you, he आदि जुड़े हों), possessive pronoun तय करने के लिए:
- अगर सभी मिलकर एक group बना रहे हैं → **Plural possessive** use करें (जैसे our)
  - *I, you and he should do our duty (together)* — जहाँ सामूहिक बात हो
- Verb का Agreement हमेशा **nearest subject** से नहीं होता — यह ध्यान से सेंटेंस के meaning अनुसार तय होता है, सिर्फ nearest word देख कर मत जोड़ो।

## 11. Relative Pronoun — Who, Whom, Which, That

### परिभाषा:
- Relative Pronoun अपने आप को **ठीक पहले वाले Noun/Pronoun** (जिसे **Antecedent** कहते हैं — Ante/Pre = पहले) से **Relate** करता है।
- यह किसी **Noun के लिए भी और Pronoun के लिए भी** use हो सकता है (साधारण Pronoun सिर्फ Noun के बदले आता है, पर Relative Pronoun Pronoun के बदले भी आ सकता है)।
- यह **Conjunction** का काम भी करता है (दो sentences को जोड़ता है)।

### Who vs Whom
- **Who** → हमेशा **Subjective Case** (केवल Persons के लिए)
- **Whom** → हमेशा **Objective Case** (केवल Persons के लिए)

**पहचानने की Trick (Pairing Method — लंबे sentences में):**
1. Sentence में सारे Subjects को उनके Verbs से जोड़ो (Pair बनाओ)।
2. अगर कोई Verb बिना Subject के बचा है → वहाँ **Who** चाहिए (Subject बनाने के लिए)।
3. अगर कोई Subject या Verb नहीं बचा, सिर्फ Object की जगह खाली है → **Whom** चाहिए।

Examples:
- *The girl who helped me is my friend.* (who = Subject of "helped")
- *I met my friend who was returning from market.* (who = Subject of "was returning")
- *He loves whom* (उल्टा करके check करें — अगर Object का जवाब आ रहा है तो Whom)

### Possessive of Who → **Whose**
(जैसे He का His, It का Its, They का Their)
- Whose सिर्फ Persons के लिए use होता है।
- Non-living things के लिए possessive की जगह **"of which"** use करते हैं।
  - *This is the road, the inauguration of which was done by the President.*
  - *The Ganga is a river, the origin of which is a matter of speculation.*
- Position ध्यान रखें: "of which" उस noun के तुरंत बाद आता है जिससे वह related है (road के बाद नहीं, inauguration के बाद)।

### Relative Pronoun भी Conjunction का काम करता है
- *The teacher guided the student who was not able to take proper decisions.* (who ने sentence जोड़ भी दिया)

## 12. Which vs That

### Which
- Non-living things के लिए
- **Choice/चयन** की situation में — कई items में से एक select करना हो (चाहे persons हों या things) → **Which**
  - *There are two rooms of which one leads to the kitchen.*
  - *I do not know which of the servants stole it.* (persons के साथ भी Which चल सकता है जब choice की बात हो)

### That — इन Special Cases में हमेशा "That" use होता है (Who/Which की जगह भी):
1. जब Sentence में **Superlative Degree** आए (comparison 2 से ज्यादा में हो)
   - *He is the wisest person that I have ever met.*
   - *This is the best piece of advice that I have ever received.*
2. जब निम्न words आएँ: **All, Only, Alone, Any, Anything, Nothing, Much, Such** आदि
   - *All that glitters is not gold.*
   - *He is the only person that can help you.*
   - *There is nothing that can check me.*
3. जब Sentence में एक साथ **Person + Animal/Thing** दोनों का जिक्र हो
   - *The lady and her dog that were coming were greeted.*

**Note:** "That" तीन काम कर सकता है:
1. Relative Pronoun (जैसे ऊपर के rules में)
2. Demonstrative Adjective (*That book is very interesting.*)
3. Conjunction (*He said that he would come.*)

**पहचान का तरीका:** Relative Pronoun वाले "That" के **ठीक पहले एक Noun** आता है (Antecedent)। Conjunction वाले "That" के पहले कोई Noun नहीं होता, वह सिर्फ जोड़ने का काम करता है।

---

## 13. What vs That

**Rule (Simple Method):**
- अगर उस जगह से पहले **कोई Noun/Pronoun मौजूद है** → **That** लगाओ
- अगर उस जगह से पहले **कोई Noun/Pronoun नहीं है** → **What** लगाओ

Examples:
- *This is the book that I need.* (Noun "book" मौजूद है → That)
- *This is what I need.* (कोई Noun पहले नहीं → What)

**दूसरा Check:** Sentence को उल्टा करके पूछें — अगर किसी Noun का जवाब आ रहा है (जैसे *I need a pen/pencil*) तो **That** सही है; अगर बिना Noun के जवाब पूरा बनता है तो **What**।

---

## 14. Interrogative Pronoun

- Same words (Who, Whom, Which, What) जब **प्रश्न पूछने** के लिए use हों → **Interrogative Pronoun**
  - *Who helped you?* (यहाँ "Who" Relative नहीं, Interrogative है क्योंकि सवाल पूछा जा रहा है)

---

## 15. "One" — Possessive & Plural

- **One's** (Apostrophe वाला) → **Possessive of "One"** (जैसे He का His)
  - *One should keep one's promises.*
  - सिर्फ तभी use करें जब "One" का मतलब **"हर एक / Everybody"** (each one) के sense में हो।
- **Ones** (बिना Apostrophe) → **Plural of "One"**
  - *One of the boys did his work.* (यहाँ "one" specific एक व्यक्ति है, "each one" के sense में नहीं — इसलिए यहाँ "One's" नहीं, केवल एक साधारण possessive rule लागू होगा)

**ध्यान रहे:** "One" जब **"each one/everybody"** के meaning में Pronoun का काम करे → possessive **One's**
जब "One" किसी particular व्यक्ति के लिए हो (जैसे "one of the students") → वहाँ "each" का सेंस नहीं, इसलिए "one's" न लगाएं; बल्कि gender अनुसार he/she/his/her लगाएं।

---

## 16. It's vs Its vs Itself (Special Confusion)

- **Its** → Possessive Adjective (बिना apostrophe) — *India is proud of its culture.*
- **It's** (Apostrophe के साथ) → हमेशा **Short form** है:
  - It's = It **is**
  - या It's = It **was**
  - या It's = It **has**
  - (Context/Tense के हिसाब से तय करें — यह Possessive नहीं है)
  - Example: *It's been a year since I met her.* = It **has** been a year...

## 17. Additional Points (Previously Missed)

### (a) Alternate Names of Cases
- **Subjective Case** = also called **Nominative Case**
- **Objective Case** = also called **Accusative Case**
(Possessive Case is sometimes called Genitive Case)

### (b) "Dummy Subject"
- **Impersonal "It"** (the one that doesn't act as subject/object but just introduces the real subject) is also known as a **Dummy Subject**.
  - *It is I who help Ram.* → "It" here = Dummy Subject/Impersonal Pronoun

### (c) Fixed Prepositions with Reflexive Verbs
| Verb | Preposition |
|---|---|
| Avail | of → *He availed himself **of** the opportunity.* |
| Adapt | to → *She adapted herself **to** the situation.* |
| Reconcile | to → *He reconciled himself **to** their demand.* |
| Pride | on → *He prides himself **on** his work.* |

### (d) Who/Whom — Ambiguous Case
- *"The person I met was my friend"* type sentences can sometimes take **either who or whom**, but the **meaning shifts**:
  - **Who** (emphasis on identity): *The person who is my friend...* → focus is "which person is my friend"
  - **Whom** (emphasis on the action of meeting): *The person whom I met...* → focus is "the act of meeting"
- If the sentence continues further after this clause (e.g., *...I met yesterday*), **whom** is usually preferred; otherwise, both are grammatically acceptable depending on intended meaning.

### (e) "Every" Forces Singular Verb
- Even when two "every + noun" phrases are joined by "and", the verb remains **singular**, because "every" itself is always singular:
  - *Every man and every woman **applies**.* (not "apply")
  - Compare: *Man and woman apply* (plural, no "every") vs *Every man and every woman applies* (singular, "every" forces it)

### (f) Note on "Gotten" (tangential grammar point mentioned in class)
- ❌ *I have gotten late* 
- ✅ *I have got late*
- "Gotten" functions more as an adjective/different usage and isn't the expected form here.
