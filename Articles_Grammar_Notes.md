# Articles (A / An / The) — Exam Notes
*Source: "Article in 2 Hours" — Basic English Grammar lecture*

---

## 1. What does "Article" mean?
Three different meanings of the word:
1. A **newspaper article** — a short write-up in a paper (unrelated to grammar).
2. **Article = things/commodities** — e.g., "The articles were stolen" (सामान चोरी हो गया).
3. **Grammatical article** — a, an, the → this is what we study here.

## 2. Why is Article not counted as a separate Part of Speech?
- Articles are a part of **Adjectives** — specifically, they fall under **Determiners**.
- Determiners = a sub-class of adjectives that show **quantity/number/value** of a noun, but unlike regular adjectives they **have no degrees of comparison** (no positive–comparative–superlative forms).
  - Adjective example: good → better → best (has degrees)
  - Determiner example: *some* (no degree exists for "some")
- Since Determiners already fall under Adjectives, Article is not given a separate Part-of-Speech category.
- In **Modern Grammar**, articles are simply called **Determiners**.

## 3. The Three Articles
| Type | Articles | Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Indefinite** | a, an | Non-specific/general person or thing |
| **Definite** | the | Specific/particular, already-known person or thing |

**Exam tip (sentence rearrangement/para-jumbles):** If one sentence has "a boy" and another has "the boy," the sentence with **"a boy" comes first** (introduces the person), and **"the boy" comes after** (refers back to the same, now-specific, person).
- "The doctor" = a specific doctor already known/appointment already fixed.

## 4. Article goes with Noun OR Pronoun — not always directly with the noun
- Articles are always attached to a **noun or a pronoun**, never standing alone.
- Example: *I don't want the blue pen, I want the red one.* → "the" here modifies **"one" (a pronoun** standing in for "pen"), not just nouns directly.
- Example: *He is a foolish boy.* → "a" belongs to **"boy" (the noun)**, not to "foolish" (the adjective). If you drop "boy," you must drop "a" too: *He is foolish* (correct), NOT *He is a foolish* (wrong).
- **Rule of thumb:** the article always travels together with the noun/pronoun it belongs to — remove one, remove the other.

## 5. Singular vs Plural usage
- **A / An** → used only with **singular** nouns.
- **The** → can be used with **both singular and plural**.

## 6. A vs An — the rule is SOUND, not spelling
- Common (wrong) school-level shortcut: "vowel letters (a,e,i,o,u) → an; everything else → a." **This is not reliable at the competitive-exam level.**
- **Correct rule:** Look at the **pronunciation/sound** of the word immediately following the article — not the written letter.
  - **Vowel sound → an**
  - **Consonant sound → a**
- Classic trap words:
  - *a university* (NOT "an university") — "u" here is pronounced like "yu" (consonant /j/ sound), despite being a vowel letter.
  - *an umbrella* — genuine vowel sound, so "an."
  - *an SP* — "S" is pronounced "es" (vowel sound), so "an," even though S is a consonant letter.
  - *a son* — "s" here has a consonant sound, so "a."
  - *an MA* — "M" pronounced "em" (vowel sound) → "an."
- **Note on "the":** "The" itself is pronounced two ways depending on the next sound — "thuh" before a consonant sound, "thee" before a vowel sound. (This is about pronouncing "the," not about choosing a/an.)

## 7. Article agrees with the word immediately following it — not the "real" noun being described
- Example: *He is an intelligent boy.* → "an" is chosen because of **"intelligent"** (vowel sound), even though the article ultimately relates to "boy."
- Always check the word **right after the article**, not the head noun further down the sentence, when deciding a/an.

## 8. Uses of "The" (Definite Article)

**a) A particular/specific person or thing**
- *The boys were present.* (specific, known boys)

**b) Representing a whole class/species** (not one specific member — the entire category)
- *The dog is a useful animal.* / *The rose is the sweetest of flowers.* / *The camel is the ship of the desert.*
- **Exception:** *Man* and *Woman*, when used for the whole human class, take **no article**.
  - *Man is mortal.* (NOT "The man is mortal") — general truth about all humans.
  - (If referring to one specific man, an article would be used normally.)

**c) Before certain holy/religious books**
- the Vedas, the Quran, the Ramayana, the Bible, etc.

**d) Before certain body parts** (idiomatic use)
- the eyes, the head, the nose

**e) Before names of rivers, mountain ranges, seas, island groups**
- the Ganga, the Himalayas, the Arabian Sea, the Andamans

**f) With the Superlative Degree**
- *He is the best boy.* — comparing among many, one stands out, so "the."

**g) With Ordinals (first, second, third...)**
- *the first chapter of the book*, *on the second floor* (NOT "a second floor")

**h) With the Comparative Degree — conditional rule**
- Comparison using **"than" → NO article**: *He is wiser than his brother.*
- Comparison using **"of" → "the" IS used**: *the higher of the two*
- **"The ___, the ___" parallel-comparative structure → always takes "the"**, even with no "than"/"of":
  - *The higher you go, the colder it gets.*
  - *The more you practice, the better you become.*

## 9. Articles with Proper Nouns
- **Default rule:** No article before proper nouns (names of people/places).
  - *Shakespeare was a great dramatist.* (talking about the real, original Shakespeare — no "the")
- **Exception — when a proper noun is used to indicate a QUALITY** (i.e., the proper noun is turned into a "common noun" standing for a trait), **"the" is used**:
  - *Kalidas was the Shakespeare of India.* (Kalidas isn't literally Shakespeare — he has Shakespeare-*like* qualities)
  - *Kashmir is the Switzerland of India.*
  - *He is the Virat Kohli of our class.* / *She is the Aishwarya Rai of our class.*
- Even a person's own name can take "the" when their inherent *quality/trait* (not their identity) is being described:
  - *The judge in him awoke.* (his judge-like quality — fairness/impartiality — awakened, not an actual judge)

## 10. One Article for Two Qualities of the SAME Subject
(Parallel to the "one apostrophe for joint possession" rule in Nouns.)
- If two qualities/roles refer to the **same person**, use only **one article**, placed before the **first** noun:
  - *He introduced me to his friend, who is a singer and artist.* (one person, both a singer and an artist → single "a")
- If they refer to **two different people**, use a separate article before each:
  - *the treasurer and the secretary* (two different people)
  - vs. *the treasurer and secretary* (same person holding both posts)

## 11. Omission of Article (Where NO Article is used)

**a) Institutions used for their primary/intended purpose**
(school, college, temple, church, jail, hospital)
- *He goes to school.* (for studying — primary purpose) → no article
- *He goes to church for prayers.* → no article
- **But if NOT used for the primary purpose → "the" is used:**
  - *He went to the school to drop his brother.* (not to study)
  - *He went to the church to repair the gate.* (not to pray)
- A **specific/named** institution also takes "the": *Tihar Jail* → *the Tihar jail*

**b) Sunset, sunrise, and other exact-moment time expressions**
- *at sunset*, *at sunrise* → no article (a precise point in time)
- **But** *in the morning*, *in the evening*, *in the afternoon* → **DO** take "the" (these are broader time spans, not a single instant)
- *Today, yesterday, tomorrow* → these are adverbs, so they never take an article at all.

**c) General-sense statements (not specific) → no article**
- *Man is mortal.* (general truth) vs. *The man I saw in the forest...* (specific)
- *Water is essential for life.* (general) vs. *The water of this river is polluted.* (specific)
- *Gold is a precious metal.* (general) vs. *The gold in this ring is not pure.* (specific)

**d) Names of most countries → no article**
- *India* (NOT "the India")
- **Exception:** Countries/territories formed by a **union** of regions **do** take "the":
  - the United States, the United Kingdom, the West Indies

**e) Names of languages → no article; but the SAME word for the PEOPLE of that nation DOES take "the"**
- *She studies English.* (language → no article)
- *The English defeated the French.* (referring to the *people*, not the language → "the")

**f) Certain idiomatic phrases — fixed, no article**
- in difficulty · by mistake · in detail · in anger
- **Contrast (takes an article):** in *a* dilemma · in *a* bad mood

| Next word starts with a consonant **sound** | a |
| Specific/known person or thing | the |
| Superlative degree | the |
| Ordinal numbers (first, second...) | the |
| Comparative + "of" / "the...the..." pattern | the |

## High-Yield Exam Traps to Remember
- Judge a/an by **sound**, never by the written letter (university, SP, MA type words).
- Article's a/an choice depends on the **word right after it**, not the final noun.
- "The" for institutions/sunrise-sunset only when NOT used in the primary/exact sense.
- Man/Woman in general sense = **no article** (exception to the "whole class" rule).
- Comparative with "than" = no article; comparative with "of" or in "the...the..." pattern = "the."
